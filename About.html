About William Mahaney

Hello! I am William Mahaney, a mathematician specializing in applied topics in algebra and number theory. My research primarily revolves around advancing the fields of isogeny cryptography and code-based cryptography; both are types of post-quantum cryptography, cryptographic systems resistant to attacks from both classical and quantum computers.

Isogeny cryptography involves leveraging isogenies between elliptic curves for cryptographic purposes.

Code-based cryptography involves utilizing error-correcting codes for developing cryptographic algorithms.

I am passionate about advancing communications technology by designing and refining systems and algorithms used to perform daily tasks. My goal is to contribute innovative solutions that ensure the confidentiality, integrity, speed, and efficacy of communications systems.

Outside of my research, I write expositional documents on topics in Algebra, Number Theory, and Algebraic Geometry. I also attend research conferences and collaborate with other researchers to produce research articles. You can find my preprints and journal publications in my Research Papers page.
